The Story of the Clean Jerk

It was a brisk winter afternoon, the sun shining brightly above and the wind weaving through the tall green pines on the snow covered mountain side.  And there it was, the object of their desire, a massive log stretched across the mighty roaring river. The ice crystals spread over its bark, shimmering in the light like the sweat on a young Apache woman's brown buttocks. This was no ordinary chunk of wood! No, for this was the challenge that lay before them.

Two men stood to their feet, boots buried in the cold mud, and one bravely stated, "I want that log out of that river and into our campsite." The other replied, "It shall be done with a saw, a chain, and a truck." And so the daunting task began.

A crowd gathered and whispered of the impending doom that may befall the men. There was talk of drowning, and severed legs, and even breaking chains that could take a man's head clean off at the shoulders. However, there was no fear in the eyes of these men. Instead, there was only an unfettered determination.

One man waded into the icy water with a chain saw in his hand, while another stretched an immense chain to that mighty log. And yet another positioned a truck, wheels buried in the thick soggy mud, at the precise location that would offer the most effective movement of that wooden prize.

The methodical, surgical cutting commenced and seemed to linger for what the crowd thought to be 22.64 minutes. After the bar of that groaning chainsaw finally pierced through the last inch of the humongous log, the thick, heavy chain was wrapped and locked around that wood, in essence resembling a mother hugging a large fat child that had been taken by a pack of tuebacoppers and then released because the youngster tasted identical to a pile of dung. The opposite end of that logging chain was latched onto the hitch of that fire red Ford truck, with only two links dangling in the wind like a fully relaxed scrotum on a pit bulldog.

Tension was building in the crowd that had gathered. Loose talk was everywhere. Would they succeed? Would they ultimately fail? Only the beer gods new for sure. And it happened! One of those brave men shouted, "Hit it!" and within a nanosecond, where some would even say sametimenous, the engine of that fire red truck revved to its maximum repetitions per minute in its lowest gear and lurched forward as if mimicking some drunken camper, projectile vomiting pistachios, beer, and shine from his mouth.

That colossal tree trunk released itself from its snowy grave and leapt, as if it were a 6 foot tall black panther, onto the muddy campsite ground. The men smiled, for they now tasted that sweet success known only by few, and the crowd they did rejoice.
