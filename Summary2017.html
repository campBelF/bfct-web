The Story of the Tree Fall

It was a Thursday morning just before noon. Three campers were scurrying around the riverside campsite like roaches surprised by a bright light. There was work to be completed and these able bodied men were determined to complete their daunting tasks at breakneck speed. Why? Why were these men rushing through their jobs as if their very lives depended upon it? It was because of that cruel, vindictive bitch Mother Nature. She had unleashed upon that green laurel filled mountain a wrath so hateful and so detestable, that only 8 years of an Obama administration could even compare to such torture. This wrath, was it fire? No. Was it rain? No. Was it a landslide like the dumb-ass liberals in California experience yearly and yet continue to rebuild their houses in the same damn location? Hell no! It was WIND damn it! And this was no ordinary wind, mind you. This wind roared like a dragon with his left nut caught in a wooden dungeon door. The men, they truly pondered if the beer gods would spare their minuscule lives or would they indeed become victims of Mother Nature's vexation. But there was no time to reflect upon opportunities missed and fleeting moments that had passed in life. Instead, one camper skillfully pounded a tent stake into the ground to prevent the massive tarp from filling with wind like panty hose on a farting fat bitch. He drew back the iron headed hammer to prepare for the next mighty stroke, and their worst fear came to be a reality!

The fierce shriek of the wind storm had been answered by a splitting crash over their bewildered heads, and then a mass of falling pine tree had thundered down upon them. Through the deafening wind and forceful crashing tree, the camper shouted his warning of the current danger to the other two. The men swiftly recognized the formidable peril and scattered quicker than a Kansas City faggot accidentally walking into a biker bar. This gigantic beast of a tree had narrowly missed them all and their poignant lives had this day been spared by the beer gods. As their escalated heart rates began to regain some semblance of normality, they began to regrettably assess the damage before them. A gargantuan pine now lay directly through the center of the campsite, its branches reaching out in all directions as if to express puny ass humans had best get out of its path. And then, through blury eyes the unthinkable came into view. Vehicles once whole, once pure, once intact were now the true victims of that vicious bitch Mother Nature. Two innocent trucks smashed, smashed like two yard bird eggs tossed from the 4th story apartment balcony at 101 Riverside on East North Street. A shaking voice could barely be heard exclaiming, "You oughta see your truck, Lusk"! And a quick but loud retort was conveyed, "Haaa, you orta see YOUR f*****g truck"!

Yes, vehicles were smashed to shit and the damage would be in the thousands of dollars. But those brave men would drive out of that campsite on Sunday with their heads held high, knowing they survived Mother Nature's assassination attempt. Those men would live another day, and be able to tell a great, somewhat imbelished story, to their friends and family.
